> Values
July 20, 2014

In this blog post I'll reflect on some of the values that are most important to me, rate how I try to live up to them, and explore one in particular and why it is of importance to me. I will also reflect on a time I was asked for advice and how I might give myself advice on mediating self-doubt during DBC.

When I think of a time in my life when I have been the happiest, proudest, or most satisfied, the following values come to mind. In parenthesis, I have noted on a scale from 1 (not very) to 5 (very) of how successful I am in living up to these values. Generally, I think all of these values are really important to me, but some come more naturally to me, while I need to work on others.

  • Honesty(4)
  • Initiative(4)
  • Accountability(3)
  • Inquisitiveness(3)
  • Empathy(5)
  • Enthusiasm(4)
  • Acceptance(5)

> Enthusiasm!

When I reflect on the happiest and most satisfied periods in my life, I think enthusiasm was one of the most important values that I embraced. Enthusiasm is seizing the day and taking a positive attitude. I can often have a tendency to be overwhelmed by a full schedule, but when I remember to embrace enthusiasm, I remind myself that all of the things I fill my life with are things I want to or should be doing. I remember to live in the moment and to enjoy what I'm doing. Being enthusiastic about what I like doing makes me enjoy it even more, and being enthusiastic about things I may not be excited about helps me find the positive in every situation.

> Advice

The last thing someone asked for my advice on was how to navigate a work conversation to approach our manager about a concern. I think this person looked to me for advice because I am very honest, so I told him exactly what I thought. I also think I'm pretty empathetic, which helps me see both sides and both perspectives, even if I may not agree with one or the other. I was therefore able to give him some advice on how our manager might interpret his concerns and what kind of appeals would be most successful.

> Values and the Stereotype Threat

My values are characteristics that I value in myself and in others. I feel like they encompass the virtues that I aspire to - I do not always live up to them, but I do not feel I fall too far short and do not beat myself up if I don't live up to them. I think each of these values can help me mediate stereotype threat by giving me perspective on my goals and what's important.

I think enthusiasm will be especially important when I may be feeling down at DBC. If I am discouraged, tired, or frustrated, I will need to remember that I really do love what I'm doing and that I want to keep doing it.
