Chicago Public Schools tracks key metrics for students and schools across the school district. Schools are rated according to their school-level metrics. These metrics and the school rates are provided in Annual Reports. School-level metrics are also provided in download-able spreadsheets.


Annual Reports

School Progress Reports

The School Progress Report contains key metrics for the school with two years of data for most metrics. Metrics include test score national attainment percentiles and growth percentiles, graduation rates, college enrollment rates, dropout rates, and attendance rates. The report also includes the results of the 5Essentials Survey, which is administered to students, parents, and teachers at the school each year.

The School Progress Report is located on each school's CPS school page under Progress Reports. This page can be accessed by searching the school on Find a School.

Performance Policy and School Quality Rating Policy (SQRP)

Previous to the 2013-2014 School Year, schools in CPS were rated on the "Performance Policy". Schools received a rating of Level 1, 2, or 3, and this rating determined the degree to which the School Board could intervene in the operation and administration of the school. In the 2013-2014 a new rating system, called the "School Quality Rating Policy" (SQRP) was implemented. Schools will now be rated as Tier 1 through Tier 5. No schools have yet to be rated according to the new policy.

The Performance Policy is located on each school's CPS school page under Reports. This page can be accessed by searching the school on Find a School.
